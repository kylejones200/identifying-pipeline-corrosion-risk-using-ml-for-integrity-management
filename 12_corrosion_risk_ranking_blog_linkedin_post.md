Pipeline integrity management requires risk ranking: which threats get addressed, and which get deferred. Traditional corrosion risk models use linear scoring, but corrosion is nonlinear. Machine learning learns these interactions from data.

This tutorial covers corrosion risk ranking: gradient boosting classifiers learning nonlinear interactions between cathodic protection, soil resistivity, and coating condition achieving ROC AUC 0.87+, joint-level risk scoring combining probability with consequence factors, and automated inspection prioritization optimizing limited budgets.

This matters because corrosion is nonlinear—the interaction between soil resistivity and CP potential isn't additive but multiplicative and threshold-driven. Machine learning captures these interactions from inline inspection results and CP surveys, producing risk rankings that focus resources where failure probability intersects with consequence.
